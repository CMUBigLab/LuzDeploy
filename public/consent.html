ONLINE CONSENT FORM

This task is part of a research study conducted by Jeffrey Bigham at Carnegie Mellon University and is funded by Carnegie Mellon University. The purpose of the research is to understand how to coordinate volunteers to perform collective action that has a positive impact on the disabled.

Procedures

If you participate, you will be asked to perform a series of simple tasks at a specific location. These tasks are placing beacons at specif locations and configuring them. Placing a beacon is as simple as sticking a beacon on the wall with Velcro. Configuration is placing a battery inside a beacon and connecting the the beacon via a smart phone given to you. When done, you will be asked to respond questions regarding your opinion of the system and on your performance. The duration of these tasks will be at least one minute and no more than one hour. You may quit after any task and be compensated proportional to the time spent.

Participant Requirements

Participation in this study is limited to individuals aged 18 and older.

Risks

The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities. As with other tasks in your daily life, you may tire or become bored completing our tasks. There is also a risk of confidentiality being breached.

Benefits

There may be no personal benefit from your participation in the study but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

Compensation & Costs

In some phases of our study, we will be collecting Bluetooth data from the beacons placed around the building. Each sample takes only about 30 seconds to collect, and you will be paid around $0.10-$0.50 per data sample collected. There will be no cost for your participation.

Confidentiality

The data captured for the research does not include any personally identifiable information about you.
